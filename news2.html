The Forces BRIDGE The Gap In Wayanad

Image Courtesy: Zee News India

- Written on August 9, 2024.

On July 30, 2024, Wayanad in Kerala was hit by massive landslides, sweeping out many villages and creating widespread destruction.

The Indian Army along with NDRF (National Disaster Response Force), put their lives at risk to save hapless people. Under challenging and perilous conditions, they worked courageously and tirelessly for 10 days to rescue people. They also provided relief and medical aid to those affected. 140 Army personnel also constructed a 120-feet long Bailey Bridge within a record time of 31 hours to intensify the rescue operations.

The IAF (Indian Air Force) helicopters are aiding the search operations in the worst-hit areas. More than 700 kg of relief material, 8 civilians and teams of SOG (Special Operations Group) have been airlifted since that ill-fated day.

When the Indian Army personnel left Wayanad after 10 days, they were given a heartwarming and emotional farewell by the locals. They expressed their gratitude and appreciation for the Indian Army.

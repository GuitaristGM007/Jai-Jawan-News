From Talwar to Dost: Operations That Enriched The Future Of India

Image Courtesy: ANI

- Written on August 10, 2024.

The Indian Armed Forces are forerunners when it comes to help and rescue the calamity-struck sufferers. While the Army guards the motherland, the Navy safeguards the waters and the Air Force keeps the eagle's eye from above.

The Navy launched an offensive operation on Pakistan's Karachi Port during Indo-Pak war of 1971. In 1999, it launched Operation Talwar to block Karachi Port. Operation Madad, Operation Rahat and Operation Sea Waves were launched during 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami. Operation Rahat II (2015), Operation Nistar (2018) and Operation Samudra Setu (2020) were launched to bring back Indian citizens stuck overseas under various conditions.

The IAF has launched various opertaions whenever the country needed them. During the First Kashmir War (1947), Sino-Indian War (1962), Second Kashmir War (1965), Bangladesh Liberation War (1971), the Indian Air Force played an important role.

The IAF launched Operation Maitri (2015) to facilitate the Indian Military's rescue and relief mission in quake-hit Nepal. Operation Bandar (2019) was launched to avenge the Pulwama terror attack. In 2023, Operation Dost was launched to help victims of Turkey and Syria earthquake.

It is due to the Armed Forces that the sovereignty and integrity of the country remain intact.
